A clothes steamer is often smarter than a traditional iron: it takes less space, heats up in seconds, and won’t scorch delicate fabrics. Even on a tight budget, you can get a steamer that banishes wrinkles from shirts, dresses, and curtains without breaking the bank. When choosing, focus on heat-up time (under a minute is ideal), water tank capacity (bigger means fewer refills but adds weight), and steam output. Most budget models deliver enough power for everyday touch-ups, but a few trade off durability or nozzle precision.
